What is spread?

Bid ask spread

When you trade a CFD instrument, the bid price is the price available to sell and the ask price is the price available to buy. The spread is the gap between those two prices.

For active traders, spread is one of the most important trading costs to understand because it affects the starting point of every new position.

  • Lower spreads can reduce the distance a trade needs to move before reaching break-even.
  • Spreads may change during volatile markets, news events or quieter trading sessions.
  • Account type, instrument and liquidity conditions can all affect final trading cost.

FAQ

Basics for spreads

Quick answers to the most common spread questions before you compare account conditions.

What is the bid price?

The bid price is the price at which you can sell an instrument. For example, if you are closing a long position, the bid price is the price used to value that closing action.

What is the ask price?

The ask price is the price at which you can buy an instrument. It is usually slightly above the bid price, and the difference between the two prices is the spread.

How do you calculate a spread?

Spread is calculated by subtracting the bid price from the ask price. In Forex, the result is commonly expressed in pips so traders can compare costs between currency pairs.

Why do spreads change?

Spreads can widen or tighten depending on market liquidity, volatility, session activity, news releases and the instrument being traded.

What is the difference between Standard and Raw spreads?

A Standard account keeps pricing simple with costs included in the spread. A Raw account is designed for tighter spreads with commission-based pricing.

How does spread affect trading?

A tighter spread can make trading costs easier to manage.
